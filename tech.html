Autonomous vehicles

What does it do?

Autonomous vehicles are vehicles that are controlled by a computer rather than a person. When dealing with autonomous vehicles there are generally 6 levels going from 0 to 5 used with 0 being fully controlled by humans and 5 being completely self-driven vehicles. The most common level car available amongst new cars is level 2, with many new cars containing things like lane assist and adaptive cruise control and the ability of these 2 functions running simultaneously gives them the level 2 classification. The highest-level vehicle currently publicly available is the level 3 Audi R8 with a couple of other level 3 vehicles on the way, which means in specific conditions, like a highway in good weather it is fully automated, however the driver must be ready to take control at any time. The most advanced automated cars currently in existence are level 4 cars which are owned and used by companies like Waymo and others. Level 4 means that they are fully autonomous in most situations but not all. The reason these cars are not fully available for the public is because the way that they work is that they are programmed to know specific areas and routes very well so therefore not viable for public use. The predictions for when level 5 cars, which are fully self-driving in all situations will be first made range anywhere from within the year with Elon Musk says Tesla is very close, to in multiple decades with experts at business Insider predicting we are probably several decade away from being able to sleep in a self-driven car across New York. However even after level 5 autonomous cars are made it will take a long time for them to become legal and even longer for them to become common. Before level 5 autonomous vehicles become legal it will most likely have to be proven that they are safer than human driven cars in almost all, if not all situations. Driverless cars are made possible by several different technologies including cameras, radars LIDAR to recognize obstacles around them, they use a cloud connection to work out weather conditions, traffic conditions and also to improve monitoring of nearby vehicles, and then finally they need the software to help make the decisions. The part that needs the most work is the software as this is the biggest difference between the level 4 cars around today and the level 5 cars of the future. The main issues with the software currently are getting it to be able to react to uncommon situations or situations out of its comfort zone for example what happens when it snows and it can no longer see the lines on the road or how it deals with roadwork and things like that.

What is the likely impact?

The invention of autonomous vehicles will very likely have a major impact on the world. The most negative impact on society will probably be the mass loss of jobs. With driverless cars being available the first users will mainly be the taxi companies, trucking companies and bus companies causing all their drivers to lose their jobs. Furthermore, once they start becoming more common for private owners then driving licenses will become unnecessary causing lots of driving instructors and testers to lose their jobs. One of the positive effects will be safety, a vast majority of car crashes are caused by human error, therefore when you take out the chance of human error it will majorly reduce the amount of accidents. Whilst this is a positive effect it also means that car insurance will not be nearly as required, and a lot of insurance companies will be forced to close causing even more people to lose their jobs. Another positive effect will be reducing the number of cars on the road, the reason this will happen is because people will be able to share cars a lot more easily and also less accidents will reduce traffic. The negative side effect of this is that it will mean the car industry will go down significantly causing even more job loss. The last negative effect will be the fact that cars will be able to be hacked giving another method for terrorism. So, in conclusion driverless cars in general will have a very positive impact on the environment and on many people's lives however it will cause the loss of many jobs.

How will this affect you?

How autonomous vehicles will affect my personal life, or the lives of my family members is almost impossible to know. If there was a definite time period on when autonomous vehicles would become available to the public, or even just when level 4 vehicles will become available for deliveries or as taxis then maybe the question would be answerable. For example, if they were to become available in the next couple of years for deliveries then they would very possibly be used by some of my relatives, on the other hand if they don’t become available for 10 plus years then the companies that we could use them for might no longer exist. That was only for level 4 vehicles but for how level 5 vehicles will affect me is even harder to guess, first of all they are probably even further away meaning don’t know what I will be doing with my life for example I may just be working from home and the only thing that will change is if I need a taxi somewhere it won't have a driver or I might have to drive around all day to different offices and having a driverless car will allow me to use my time a lot more efficiently as I will be able to work whilst driving and the same goes for everyone else I know. However whenever driverless cars do become available it will have a positive impact on most people's lives because it will reduce the number of accidents, it should reduce traffic congestion and should also improve a lot of people's productivity because they will no longer have to waste time whilst driving and will be able to work or do something else.

Cybersecurity

What is Cybersecurity?

A definition of the topic can be found at Wikipedia that is as follows:

Cybersecurity is the protection of computer systems and networks from the theft of or damage to their hardware, software, or electronic data, as well as from the disruption or misdirection of the services they provide.

A Cybersecurity professional is expected to be able to implement and advise on the latest best practices to follow for organisations and individuals who wish to protect their data and businesses from potential threats.

The state of the art in Cybersecurity is a rapidly and constantly changing boundary. It requires constant effort to maintain high standards. Individuals and societies ever increasing reliance on more and more complicated electronic systems means the detection and remediation of potential vulnerabilities is always evolving.

One of the interesting aspects of our current electronic systems is the communication can always be reduced to a simple voltage high or low signal and a clock. These two voltage states represent the binary one and zero language in which all current systems understand and communicate.

An actor's ability to intercept and understand this code and an opposite actor's ability to deny that ability is what drives the evolution of the field. In this way, Cybersecurity can be seen as a specific incarnation of a very human activity, literally going back thousands of years.

Work is constantly being done to identify flaws in systems and how to exploit or re-mediate them. For individuals, the threats currently often involve ransom-ware or the stealing of financial details. In these cases, the threat actor seeks to sell or extort their victim for money.

For organisations and businesses, we are currently seeing increasing efforts to steal intellectual property. In these cases, the attackers wish to remain anonymous for as long as possible so the intellectual property can be leveraged for a competitive advantage. Fig 1 Source: Accenture.com As can be seen from the chart above, ransom-ware and malicious individuals inside an organisation present the two largest threats.

Stealing the data of the target is the most common and expensive outcome from Cybersecurity failures. However, it is worth noting that it is now becoming more common for attackers to attempt to alter or destroy data of a target. The aims of these types of attacks are to make it more difficult to trust the integrity of the information being protected.

As electronic means of protection become more sophisticated, so do the attack vectors. Criminals are adapting to these changes by targeting the weakest link in the Cybersecurity chain – the human. As people store more personal information on-line via services like social media, attacks known as phishing are becoming more common. These attacks involve attempting to impersonate an individual or company in order to deceive a person into divulging sensitive information.

What are the impacts?

It is very difficult to determine accurate dollar figures for the cost of Cybercrime. As was mentioned above, often it is very difficult to discover, especially when the attacker does not wish to publicise a break of security.

The estimates of the dollar value of cybercrime are all over the place and vary widely. As an example, some current estimates of the damage in the US range from the billions to the trillions of dollars. However, TechRepublic reports that the average security budget for companies in the US has risen to $18.9 million dollars in 2019, up from $8.9 million in 2018. The cost of a corporate data breach is around $1.4 million US dollars per breach. The report also details how companies that have a security operations centre report the cost of a breach being half the average amount.

The SSL Store reports that the cybercrime economy is at least $1.5 trillion US dollars. The market for Cybersecurity products is predicted to reach $300 billion US dollars over the next few years.

What can be done?

Research into this topic is constantly evolving. Microsoft reports that up to 70% of the flaws in its software projects are due to memory safety issues. Fig2 Source: ZDNet More modern memory safe languages like Rust are working to replace older languages where these types of errors are more common.

Education is commonly cited as one solution to reduce the threats from cybercrime. As we have noted previously, the human factor is often the weak link. Password managers are a popular tool to improve personal security as well as two factor authentication. However, it is worth noting some rather bleak statistics

  • 71% of accounts are protected by passwords used on multiple websites
  • A single password is used to access five accounts on average
  • 60% of people include a name or date of birth in the on-line account passwords
  • Only 45% of users change their password after a data breach
  • 25% of people admit to using passwords like “123456” and ‘qwerty’
    • Source: Dataprot statistics

Summary

As has been shown, the size of the problem is only getting larger as more of our lives exist in a digital space. The rewards for bad actors therefore increases and our need to exhibit better security practices grow.

It is incumbent on all individuals to take it upon themselves to educate and protect themselves on-line. The costs of mitigation provide a positive return on the investment. My hope for the future is that we are currently in a stage of social development where we are still working out the benefits and limits of rapidly changing technology. Hopefully, future generations will grow up already inoculated to many of the downsides of a digital world.

For now and into the foreseeable future, the need and therefore opportunities for cybersecurity professionals will continue to grow. It is an exciting time to be involved in one of the most competitive areas of computing today.

Blockchain Technology and Cryptocurrencies

What does it do?

Blockchain is a distributed database existing on multiple computers at the same time. It is constantly growing as new sets of recordings, or 'blocks', are added to it. In simple terms, a blockchain is a way of passing information from A to B in a fully automated and safe manner. When a transaction is made, one party starts the process and a network of nodes validates the transaction and the user's status through algorithms. Once verified a block is created. This block is then verified by computers and added to a chain that is stored across the net. Now the transaction is completed and because of the blockchain it is permanent and unalterable. What makes it so great is that it's free, a blockchain can transfer and store money and in a sense replace processes that rely on a small fee for transactions. A blockchain is what enables the existence of cryptocurrencies. They also have potential applications that go beyond cryptocurrencies, these include; manage fractional ownership in autonomous vehicles, save billions of dollars on transaction costs, the ability to cast votes from a smartphone and make it safer for patients encrypted health information to be shared with multiple providers.

Cryptocurrency is a digital asset, designed to work as a means of exchange which uses cryptographic functions to conduct financial transactions. Anyone can exchange real currency for cryptocurrency and once you own it is like any form of payment that can be exchanged online for goods. However, cryptocurrency is secured by cryptography, which makes it nearly impossible to counterfeit or double-spend. The key thing that sets cryptocurrency apart from any other currency is that because of the decentralized nature of the blockchain it is not controlled by any central authority, making the currency immune to any form of government control. What this means is; the currencies supply is not determined by a central bank, the currency has no physical form like cash and only exists digitally and it has no intrinsic value, meaning it can be used for another commodity such as gold. Transactions are verified by people called cryptocurrency miners and then they are added to the public ledger. Miners use powerful computers to solve complex math problems. Mining is an open source which means anyone can confirm any transaction and the first miner that solves the problem gets to add the block to their transaction ledger. This miner is then rewarded by being able to authorise the transaction and in return for the service they earn small amounts of cryptocurrency. The original and most commonly used form of cryptocurrency is Bitcoin and there are currently 16.8 million Bitcoin in circulation.

Cryptocurrencies are still in the very early stages but they are already starting to transform the global economy. Transactions of cryptocurrencies do not require any connection to any dollar, this adds another avenue for financial transactions that participate in the global economy but at the same time also avoid traditional economic policies. This has the potential to impact governments because traditional dollars are the reserve currency for the global economy and are commonly recognised as the main source of power. Cryptocurrencies are also likely to have an effect on middlemans, potentially completely cutting them out. Unlike our traditional currencies, cryptocurrency transitions are decentralized and don’t require a third party. This has the potential to impact third parties like banks, as cryptocurrencies eliminate the need for their service. Blockchain technology has great potential for impacts in the world of cybersecurity. Standard centralized servers are likely to see; loss of data, corruption and hacking. However if a decentralized blockchain is used it would allow for data storage in the cloud to be more robust and protected against attacks. This is likely to impact that way we look at cybersecurity and have for the potential to one day completely defend against unwanted attacks. This is because of the fact that they are unable to control the currencies and there is no need for a middle man when using cryptocurrencies.
